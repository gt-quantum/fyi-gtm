## What is Clay?

Clay is a data automation platform that consolidates over 150 premium data sources and AI research agents into a single interface for lead enrichment and workflow automation. The platform addresses the common problem of fragmented data tools by creating a centralized hub where go-to-market teams can gather prospect information, automate research tasks, and trigger revenue-generating workflows based on enriched data insights.

## Decision Snapshot

- **Best for:** Go-to-market teams, sales operations professionals, and growth-focused businesses seeking comprehensive data enrichment
- **Not ideal for:** Non-technical sales reps, budget-conscious small businesses, teams needing simple single-source enrichment
- **Typical team size:** 10-100+ employees with dedicated operations support
- **Time to value:** 2-4 weeks with proper setup and training
- **Budget reality:** $1,600-2,500+ annually for meaningful usage levels

## Quick Facts

- **Category:** Data Enrichment & Hygiene
- **Pricing model:** Freemium with credit-based usage
- **Standout capability:** Marketplace approach combining 150+ data providers in one platform

## What the Tool Does

Clay functions as a data orchestration platform that pulls live information from multiple sources simultaneously rather than relying on a single database. Users create enrichment workflows that automatically gather contact details, company firmographics, intent signals, and custom research data points. The platform monitors prospect activities like job changes, website visits, and online mentions, then triggers automated actions like CRM updates or outreach campaigns based on predefined conditions.

The day-to-day workflow typically involves setting up enrichment sequences that run automatically, reviewing AI-generated research findings, and managing credit allocation across different data providers. Teams can create high-intent buyer segments by combining signals from multiple sources, then route qualified prospects directly to sales representatives or marketing automation systems.

## Key Capabilities

### Multi-Source Data Enrichment
Clay aggregates data from over 150 premium providers, allowing users to waterfall through multiple sources to maximize contact coverage. This approach addresses the common issue where single providers might only have 30-40% coverage for specific prospect lists. The platform automatically moves to the next provider if the first source lacks the requested information, significantly improving overall enrichment rates.

### AI-Powered Research Agents
The platform includes AI agents that can search public databases, analyze webpages and PDFs, and conduct Google searches to find unique data points about prospects or companies. These agents can gather information that traditional enrichment tools miss, such as recent company announcements, technology stack changes, or executive background details that inform personalized outreach strategies.

### Intent Signal Monitoring
Clay tracks behavioral signals indicating purchase intent, including job changes at target accounts, website visitor identification, and online mention tracking. The platform can monitor when prospects visit specific pages, engage with content, or when their companies appear in relevant news or discussions, providing sales teams with timely engagement opportunities.

## Pricing Reality

Clay operates on a credit-based system where different data providers and enrichment actions consume varying amounts of credits. The free tier provides 1,200 credits annually with unlimited users, suitable for light testing but insufficient for regular business use.

- **Entry cost:** $134 monthly for 24,000 credits annually (Starter plan)
- **Scaling cost:** $206 monthly for 36,000 credits, with enterprise pricing for larger volumes
- **Contract terms:** Monthly or annual billing with credit rollover capabilities

The credit consumption model means actual costs depend heavily on usage patterns and data source selection. Premium providers like ZoomInfo or Apollo consume more credits per enrichment than basic sources, making budget planning crucial for sustained usage.
